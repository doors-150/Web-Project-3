WHERE IS TREATY 3?

Treaty 3 takes up most of eastern Ontario and a little bit of west Manitoba. Compared to the sizes of other treaties, it is somewhat small. There are many communities here, including but not limited to:

  • Animakee Wa Zhing 37 First Nation
  • Buffalo Point First Nation
  • Gakijiwanong Anishinaabe Nation
  • Lac des Mille Lacs First Nation
  • Migisi Sahgaigan First Nation
  • Mitaanjigamiing First Nation
  • Naongashiing First Nation
  • Nigigoonsiminikaaning First Nation
  • Asubpeechoseewagong First Nation
  • Couchiching First Nation
  • Iskatewizaagegan 39 Independent First Nation
  • Lac Seul First Nation
  • Mishkosiminiziibiing First Nation
  • Naicatchewanin First Nation
  • Naotkamegwanning First Nation
  • Niisaachewan First Nation

WHAT WAS INCLUDED IN THIS TREATY

The historic terms of treaty 3 were that any indigenous family of five or more would get one square mile of land which could be used for farms and reserves. They could also fish and hunt for food on those lands. The people living there would also get paid five dollars (which was a lot at the time) yearly every year. And schools would be established in those areas, so indigenous kids could go there to learn. In exchange for all of this, the Canadian Government would be able to control the lands and the people.
